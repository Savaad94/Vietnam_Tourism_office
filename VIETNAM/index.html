Discover Vietnam

The emerald of Southeast Asia

Explore a land of emerald landscapes and timeless traditions. From the majestic Ha Long Bay to vibrant cities and serene rice terraces, Vietnam offers unforgettable adventures. Discover the beauty, culture, and warmth of Southeast Asia’s hidden gem.

Welcome to the Tourism Office

The Tourism office of Vietnam is dedicated to promoting the country's rich cultural, historical, and natural heritage. Our mission is to inspire the world to discover Vietnam’s treasures while supporting sustainable tourism and preserving traditions.

With an emphasis on innovation and global collaboration, we work to deliver unforgettable experiences for travelers while enhancing our economy and protecting our heritage.

Why Choose Vietnam?

A Land Where Nature, Culture, and Heart Unite

Vietnam is a land where nature steals the spotlight. Imagine emerald bays dotted with limestone towers, endless rice terraces climbing misty mountains, hidden caves echoing with silence, and untouched beaches stretching into golden sunsets. Whether you’re trekking in Sapa or cruising Ha Long Bay, Vietnam’s landscapes are a living postcard, made for those who crave wonder and awe.


Culturally, Vietnam is a masterpiece in motion. Centuries of heritage flow through ancient temples, French colonial buildings, and vibrant street festivals. In Hue, imperial palaces whisper tales of dynasties; in lantern-lit Hoi An, the past glows in every step. It’s a country where every city is a chapter, and every alley tells a story.


But it’s the people who turn your trip into a journey. Warm, resilient, and endlessly welcoming, Vietnamese locals greet you with sincere smiles and a willingness to connect. From bustling markets to remote villages, there’s a quiet beauty in how modern life and deep-rooted tradition coexist with grace.


And then there's the food—fresh, bold, and unforgettable. Vietnam is a paradise for your palate, where every street corner offers something delicious. From fragrant bowls of pho to sizzling banh xeo and sweet, iced ca phe, the cuisine is a feast of textures and flavors that reflect the soul of the country. Eating here isn’t just a pleasure—it’s a cultural experience.

Discover Vietnam’s Epic Past

Empires, Resistance, and Rebirth

Vietnam’s history stretches back over 4,000 years, beginning with the semi-mythical Hồng Bàng Dynasty. Early Vietnamese societies developed agriculture, bronze casting, and strong local identities in the Red River Delta.

From 111 BC, Vietnam was under Chinese rule for nearly a millennium. During this time, Confucianism, Buddhism, and Chinese administration shaped Vietnamese culture—but resistance never ceased. National heroes like the Trưng Sisters and Lê Lợi led famous uprisings, forging a deep-rooted sense of independence.

By the 10th century, Vietnam gained autonomy, entering a golden age of dynastic rule. The Lý, Trần, and Lê dynasties built temples, expanded southward, and defended against Mongol invasions. Huế became the imperial capital under the Nguyễn Dynasty in the 19th century, showcasing refined Vietnamese court culture.

French colonization began in the mid-1800s, introducing Western education, Christianity, and exploitation. The struggle for independence intensified until Hồ Chí Minh declared the Democratic Republic of Vietnam in 1945.

After defeating France in 1954, Vietnam was divided into North and South. The ensuing Vietnam War (1955–1975), a Cold War battleground, ended with the fall of Saigon and reunification under a communist regime.

In the decades since, Vietnam has pursued economic reforms (Đổi Mới), opened to tourism and global trade, and grown into a dynamic, modern nation—while honoring its layered heritage.

Celebrate Vietnam’s Vibrant Festivals

Vietnam’s calendar is filled with colorful, cultural celebrations that offer tourists a chance to experience the country’s rich traditions and joyful spirit. From grand processions and firework displays to lantern-filled nights and ancestral ceremonies, each festival tells a story of identity, resilience, and heritage.

These events are not just local traditions—they're invitations to connect with Vietnamese culture on a deeper level. Whether you're watching the dragon dances of Tết or strolling under lantern-lit skies in Hoi An, Vietnam’s festivals are unforgettable experiences that blend celebration and meaning.

Tết (Lunar New Year)

The biggest celebration in Vietnam, filled with fireworks, family rituals, and colorful markets.

Mid-Autumn Festival

Also known as the Lantern Festival, it's a magical night of mooncakes, glowing lanterns, and family fun.

Whale Festival

Held in coastal towns to honor the whale spirit protector, featuring sea-themed rituals and vibrant parades.

Hue Festival

A biennial celebration of Vietnam's imperial culture, with parades, traditional music, and art performances.

Cham Fire Dance

An intense, spiritual ritual from the Cham people involving music, firewalking, and ancient chants.

Trưng Sisters Festival

A celebration of Vietnam’s legendary heroines, marked by temple ceremonies and historical reenactments.
