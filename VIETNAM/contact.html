How to get from differents part of the world

United States

How to get there: Travelers from the U.S. can reach Vietnam via one-stop connections through major hubs like Tokyo, Seoul, Taipei, or Doha. Common arrival cities include Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh City, and Da Nang.

Documentation: U.S. citizens must obtain an e-visa in advance, allowing stays up to 90 days. The process is online and generally completed within 3–5 business days.

Vaccines: None are mandatory, but hepatitis A, hepatitis B, typhoid, and routine vaccines (MMR, DTP, influenza) are strongly recommended.

Europe

How to get there: Direct or one-stop flights from major cities like Paris, Frankfurt, Amsterdam, or London are available. Stopovers typically occur in Doha, Dubai, or Bangkok.

Documentation: Most EU citizens require an e-visa, but countries like France, Germany, and Italy have a 15-day visa exemption for tourism.

Vaccines: No required shots, but hepatitis A, B, typhoid, and Japanese encephalitis (for rural visits) are advised.

Indonesia

How to get there: Direct flights connect Jakarta, Bali, and Surabaya to Vietnamese cities like Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi via low-cost carriers and national airlines.

Documentation: Indonesian citizens enjoy visa-free entry for up to 30 days.

Vaccines: No vaccines are required, but hepatitis A, typhoid, and tetanus are recommended.

Continental Asia

How to get there: Short, direct flights are available from cities in China, Japan, South Korea, Thailand, and Malaysia. Budget airlines and regional carriers offer daily routes.

Documentation: Some Asian countries (e.g., Singapore, Japan, South Korea) qualify for visa waivers, while others need an e-visa.

Vaccines: Not mandatory, but hepatitis A, B, typhoid, and Japanese encephalitis (for rural areas) are advisable.

Australia

How to get there: Flights from Sydney, Melbourne, or Brisbane often connect via Singapore or Kuala Lumpur, with some direct options to Ho Chi Minh City and Hanoi.

Documentation: Australians must apply online for an e-visa valid up to 90 days.

Vaccines: No shots are required, but it’s wise to get hepatitis A, B, typhoid, and consider Japanese encephalitis for rural travel.

Latin America

How to get there: Expect two or more connections from major cities like Mexico City, São Paulo, or Buenos Aires. Common stopovers are in the U.S., Europe, or the Middle East.

Documentation: Most Latin American citizens must apply for an e-visa before travel, valid for up to 90 days.

Vaccines: If transiting through or coming from a yellow fever–endemic country, you must show proof of yellow fever vaccination. Also recommended: hepatitis A, typhoid, tetanus.

Russia

How to get there: Travelers from Russia can fly to Vietnam via connections in hubs like Dubai, Doha, or Istanbul. Some seasonal direct flights are available from Moscow to Hanoi or Ho Chi Minh City.

Documentation: Russian citizens must apply for an e-visa prior to travel, valid for up to 90 days.

Vaccines: No mandatory vaccines, but hepatitis A, B, typhoid, and routine immunizations are recommended.

Africa

How to get there: Travelers from major cities like Johannesburg, Nairobi, or Cairo typically connect through the Middle East or Europe en route to Vietnam.

Documentation: Most African nationalities require an e-visa, valid for up to 90 days, applied for online.

Vaccines: Yellow fever vaccination may be required depending on departure country. Recommended: hepatitis A, typhoid, tetanus.

Canada

How to get there: Flights from Toronto, Vancouver, or Montreal usually connect in cities like Seoul, Tokyo, or Doha before reaching Vietnam.

Documentation: Canadian citizens need to apply for an e-visa before arriving, valid for stays up to 90 days.

Vaccines: No mandatory vaccines. Recommended: hepatitis A, B, typhoid, and routine immunizations.

Middle East

How to get there: Direct or one-stop flights from cities like Dubai, Doha, Abu Dhabi, and Riyadh are widely available, with daily connections to Hanoi and Ho Chi Minh City.

Documentation: Most Middle Eastern travelers must apply for an e-visa online, valid for up to 90 days.

Vaccines: No mandatory vaccines, but hepatitis A, typhoid, and routine vaccines are strongly recommended.
